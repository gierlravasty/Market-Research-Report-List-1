<p><p>The In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) market comprises four main types: </p><p>1. **Fresh Non-donor**: Embryos created using a woman's own fresh eggs, commonly used in standard IVF procedures. </p><p>2. **Frozen Non-donor**: Embryos derived from previously harvested eggs that are cryopreserved for later use. </p><p>3. **Fresh Donor**: Embryos created using eggs from a fresh donor, allowing recipients without viable eggs to conceive. </p><p>4. **Frozen Donor**: Similar to fresh donor, but utilizes previously frozen donor eggs, providing flexibility in timing for recipients.</p></p>
