Rapier looms utilize a flexible rapier to insert weft yarn, making them suitable for various fabrics. Air-jet looms use bursts of air to propel the yarn, ideal for lightweight materials. Water jet looms employ high-pressure water to carry the yarn, best for specific fiber types. Tissue looms, a subtype, are tailored for lightweight fabrics like tissue and non-woven materials, maximizing production speed and versatility.</p></p>
