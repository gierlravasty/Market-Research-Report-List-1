<p><p>The textile dyestuff market is categorized into several types based on their chemical properties and application processes. Disperse dyes are primarily used for synthetic fibers, offering vibrant colors. Reactive dyes bond with fibers, ideal for cotton and cellulose materials. Sulfur dyes are cost-effective and suited for dark shades on cotton. Vat dyes provide excellent fastness, suitable for cotton and cellulosic fibers. Acid dyes are used for protein fibers like wool and silk. Other dyes encompass specialty dyes for various applications.</p></p>
